# UMBL Research Topic List (English Edition)

The following topics are suitable for undergraduate theses, course papers, or independent research projects.  
All topics are aligned with UMBL’s identity as a pictographic language‑design development project.

---

## 1. Systematic Analysis of UMBL Character‑Formation Principles  
Study UMBL’s rules for constructing characters and combining components.

## 2. Structure and Classification of Pictographic Morphemes  
Explore how pictographic morphemes can be defined, categorized, and organized.

## 3. Combination Logic of Pictographic Words in UMBL  
Analyze how components form “words” and evaluate semantic transparency.

## 4. Feasibility Study of Preliminary Grammar Prototypes  
Attempt to construct simple grammar structures and test comprehensibility.

## 5. Structural Comparison Between UMBL and Chinese Character Components  
Compare formation logic, semantic transparency, and compositionality.

## 6. Comparison of UMBL with Esperanto, Lojban, and Other Conlangs  
Analyze differences in design philosophy, transparency, and learnability.

## 7. Study of Evolution Rules for UMBL  
Investigate how to design maintainable and extensible evolution mechanisms.

## 8. Learning Curve Experiments for a Pictographic Language  
Evaluate learning speed and difficulty through user studies.

## 9. Cognitive Differences Between Pictographic and Linear Text Expressions  
Study differences in comprehension speed, memory load, and processing.

## 10. Semantic Coverage Analysis of the UMBL Component Repository  
Analyze the semantic range, distribution, and gaps in the component set.

## 11. Evaluation of Visual Consistency and Readability in UMBL  
Assess visual coherence and readability from design and cognitive perspectives.

## 12. Experimental Construction of Pictographic Sentences Using UMBL  
Build simple sentences and test cross‑cultural comprehensibility.

## 13. Encoding Schemes and Data Structures for UMBL  
Explore computer‑readable representations of pictographic language.

## 14. Automated Composition Algorithms for Pictographic Expressions  
Design algorithms to automatically combine components into expressions.

## 15. Prototype Design of an Editor or Renderer for UMBL  
Develop a simple editor or rendering tool for UMBL expressions.

## 16. Semantic Transparency Comparison Between UMBL and ISO Pictograms  
Compare their effectiveness in public information communication.

## 17. Potential Role of UMBL in Cross‑Cultural Understanding  
Investigate whether pictographic language reduces cross‑cultural barriers.

## 18. Maintainability of UMBL Character‑Formation Rules  
Analyze consistency and scalability of formation rules during expansion.

## 19. Designer Experience Study on Constructing Pictographic Units  
Interview or test designers to understand challenges in constructing units.

## 20. User Testing of Semantic Transparency in UMBL  
Evaluate intuitiveness and misinterpretation rates through experiments.

---

These topics can be further refined based on student interests, course requirements, or methodological preferences.  
UMBL’s openness and experimental nature provide rich opportunities across linguistics, cognitive science, information design, and computer science.
